Early Life

Joseph Smith was born on December 23, 1805, in Sharon, Vermont, USA. Raised in a farming family, he experienced religious revivals and fervor during the "Second Great Awakening" period.

Religious Experiences

In 1820, Smith reported a theophany known as the "First Vision," claiming God the Father and Jesus Christ appeared to him. This event became foundational for the LDS Church.

In 1823, Smith said an angel named Moroni directed him to golden plates containing the Book of Mormon, which he translated and published in 1830.

Founding of the LDS Church

On April 6, 1830, Joseph Smith officially organized The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. He led the Church through migrations and growth despite persecution.

Controversial Teachings and Practices

  • Polygamy: Smith introduced plural marriage, which was practiced secretly during his life.
  • Temple Ordinances: Smith established sacred ceremonies including the endowment.
  • Prophecies: He claimed revelations guiding Church governance and doctrine.

Death and Legacy

Joseph Smith was killed by a mob in Carthage, Illinois, in 1844 while jailed on charges related to the destruction of a printing press critical of him. His death led to succession struggles and the eventual migration of many members west to Utah.

Today, Smith remains a deeply revered figure in the LDS Church, while critics question aspects of his character and history.
