Founding and Early Years (1805-1844)

  • 1805: Joseph Smith born in Vermont.
  • 1820: First Vision experience reported by Joseph Smith.
  • 1827: Smith claims to receive golden plates from Angel Moroni.
  • 1830: Publication of the Book of Mormon and official organization of the LDS Church.
  • 1831-1839: Church moves to Ohio, Missouri (with conflict and expulsion), and finally to Nauvoo, Illinois.
  • 1844: Joseph Smith killed in Carthage Jail.

Migration and Expansion (1846-1890)

  • 1846-1847: Mormon pioneers migrate west, settling in the Salt Lake Valley.
  • 1850s-1880s: Polygamy openly practiced, leading to conflicts with U.S. government.
  • 1890: Manifesto issued, officially ending polygamy.

Modernization and Global Growth (1890-Present)

  • 1978: Revelation extends priesthood to all worthy male members regardless of race.
  • 1990s-2020s: Global missionary efforts expand worldwide; temples built globally.
  • 2018: New emphasis on family history work and youth engagement.
