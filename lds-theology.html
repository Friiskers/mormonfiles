Core Doctrines

The theology of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (LDS Church) blends Christian doctrines with distinctive beliefs revealed through Joseph Smith and subsequent leaders. Some key doctrines include:

  • Godhead: The Godhead consists of three distinct beings—God the Father, Jesus Christ, and the Holy Ghost—united in purpose but separate in personhood.
  • Plan of Salvation: Life is a pre-mortal existence, followed by mortal life, then a post-mortal spirit world, resurrection, and final judgment leading to degrees of glory.
  • Restoration: The original church established by Jesus Christ was lost after the apostolic age and restored through Joseph Smith.
  • Continuing Revelation: God continues to reveal truths through modern prophets and apostles.
  • Scriptures: The Bible (King James Version), the Book of Mormon, Doctrine and Covenants, and Pearl of Great Price are considered scripture.

Distinctive Beliefs

  • Pre-Mortal Existence: Humans existed as spirit children of God before birth.
  • Exaltation: The potential for humans to become like God through obedience and temple ordinances.
  • Baptism for the Dead: Proxy baptisms performed in temples for deceased ancestors.
  • Temple Worship: Sacred ordinances including endowment and sealing performed in temples.
  • Word of Wisdom: Health code prohibiting alcohol, tobacco, coffee, and tea.

Comparisons with Mainstream Christianity

While LDS theology shares many commonalities with evangelical Christianity, such as belief in Jesus Christ’s atonement and resurrection, it differs in several key areas:

  • The nature of God as embodied beings rather than incorporeal spirits.
  • The concept of exaltation and human divinization.
  • Additional scriptures beyond the Bible.
  • Authority restored through modern prophets rather than apostolic succession.
  • Temple-specific ordinances not found in other Christian traditions.
