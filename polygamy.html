Doctrine and Covenants 132:53–66 — Problems & Biblical Contradictions

Home First Vision Joseph Smith Biography LDS Theology History Timeline Finances Controversies Bible/BOM Polygamy

Section 132 of the Doctrine and Covenants (given July 12, 1843) is among the most controversial in LDS scripture. Verses 53 through 66 describe God allegedly commanding polygamy, promising eternal exaltation for compliance, and threatening damnation for disobedience. Below, we dissect these verses and demonstrate their sharp contradictions with biblical Christianity.

Related topics: Bible vs. Book of Mormon Contradictions | Controversies | LDS Theology

📜 D&C 132:53 — “I commanded Joseph to take more wives”

Text: “Let my handmaid, Emma Smith, receive all those that have been given unto my servant Joseph...”

Problem: God allegedly commands Joseph Smith’s wife to accept his extramarital relationships. This is completely foreign to the Bible's standard of monogamous fidelity.

Biblical Contradiction: 1 Timothy 3:2, 12 — “A bishop then must be blameless, the husband of one wife...”

📜 D&C 132:54–55 — Threats of Destruction to Emma Smith

Text: God threatens Emma with destruction if she doesn’t accept Joseph’s plural wives.

Problem: This passage portrays a coercive deity issuing ultimatums based on marital compliance, which sounds closer to spiritual abuse than divine counsel.

Biblical Contradiction: 1 Corinthians 7:2–4 — Marriage is mutual and monogamous; no one is forced to share their spouse to “please God.”

📜 D&C 132:61–63 — Rules for Taking More Virgins

Text: Specifies that a man may take multiple virgins “without the first’s consent,” and they are to be “vows and covenants bound to him.”

Problem: This creates a template for non-consensual polygamy, contradicts biblical marriage as a union of equals, and suggests women exist for male exaltation.

Biblical Contradiction: Genesis 2:24 — “A man shall... be joined to his wife [singular], and they shall become one flesh.”

📜 D&C 132:63 — “To multiply and replenish the earth”

Text: Justifies polygamy as a means of “multiplying and replenishing the earth” and bringing souls into the “kingdom of God.”

Problem: God never commands polygamy for this purpose in the Bible; in fact, He warns against kings taking many wives (Deut 17:17).

Biblical Contradiction: Malachi 2:15 — God desires “godly offspring” through marital faithfulness, not quantity via multiple women.

📜 D&C 132:64–66 — God Authorizes Destruction for Rebellion

Text: The Lord says those who reject this “law” (i.e., polygamy) will be “destroyed” and “damned.”

Problem: The idea that salvation is conditional on polygamy is completely foreign to New Testament grace.

Biblical Contradiction: Galatians 1:6–9 — “If anyone preaches another gospel… let him be accursed.” The gospel does not require marriage to multiple women.

🔍 Summary: Why This Is Dangerous

  • ✅ D&C 132 contradicts the Bible’s teachings on monogamy, grace, mutual love, and the nature of salvation.
  • 🚫 The revelation supports coercive relationships and justifies Joseph Smith’s secretive polygamy, later condemned by Emma and denied publicly by early LDS leaders.
  • ❗ This section portrays God as harsh, transactional, and gender-biased—far from the God revealed in Christ.
