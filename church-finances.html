Overview

The financial operations of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (LDS Church) involve substantial income from member donations (tithing), investments, business ventures, and real estate holdings. Transparency regarding the Church’s finances has been a topic of debate both inside and outside the Church community.

Sources of Income

  • Tithing: Members voluntarily donate 10% of their income, forming the Church’s primary revenue source.
  • Investment Portfolio: The Church manages diversified assets through the Church Investment Fund, including stocks, bonds, and private equity.
  • Real Estate and Businesses: The Church owns extensive real estate, including temples, meetinghouses, farms, and commercial properties. It also owns companies like Deseret Management Corporation.
  • Other Donations: Members give to humanitarian and educational funds.

Use of Funds

The Church states funds are primarily used for:

  • Building and maintaining temples and meetinghouses worldwide.
  • Supporting missionary programs and humanitarian aid.
  • Providing education through Church-owned universities and seminaries.
  • General operating expenses of the Church’s global programs.
  • Investments aimed at long-term sustainability.

Faithful Perspectives

Church members generally see tithing as a spiritual commandment and an act of faith. They believe funds are used responsibly to further the Church’s religious mission and charitable works.

The Church periodically publishes reports about humanitarian efforts and building projects but does not disclose full financial statements publicly.

Critical Perspectives

Critics point to the Church’s immense wealth—estimated in the tens of billions of dollars—and limited financial transparency as problematic.

Investigations and reports, such as a 2019 article in The Guardian, revealed substantial real estate holdings and investments. Skeptics argue that members should be given more financial disclosure to understand how tithing is used.

Some claim the Church’s wealth contrasts with its teachings on modesty and charity.

Conclusions

LDS Church finances represent a complex balance between religious donations, business management, and investment strategy. Transparency remains a significant area of discussion both inside and outside the Church.
